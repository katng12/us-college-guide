So you wanna go to college
in the US?

Find out how

Practice Standardized Tests

Scores of standardized tests like SAT/ACT play a huge role in your US college application. Practice with books, online resources, or SAT/ACT prep classes.

Step Up Your English Game

Most of US Colleges have English proficiency requirements and often request scores of English proficiency tests like TOEFL or IELTS from international applicants. Again, practice to ace those tests.

Do Your Research

There are hundreds to thousands of colleges in the States. Do careful research to figure out good matches for your higher education based on study programs, student life, campus location, cost of attendance, and so on. Trust me this process is important and you don't want to skip it. Online resources are everywhere to be found. Make sure to check out sites like US News and College Confidential.

Be Financially Prepared

It's sad and unreasonable, but accept it. US colleges have ridiculously high costs of attendance, which can be a huge burden for international students. There are several scholarships that you can apply for, but keep in mind that most of those are pretty competitive, so make sure to polish your resume.

Be Mentally Prepared

Being an international student means that you gotta deal with numerous difficulties: living alone far from home, taking care of yourself, keeping your legal status, facing language and culture barriers, feeling lonely, and so many more not so fun stuffs. Therefore, be prepared to overcome hardships. It's not gonna be easy, but you got this!

GOOD LUCK! YOU GOT THIS.
